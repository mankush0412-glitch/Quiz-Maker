Income from house property is chargeable under:
A) Section 14
B) Section 22
C) Section 23
D) Section 24
Answer: B
Solution: Section 22 specifically deals with income from house property. Section 14 lists heads of income, Section 23 defines annual value, Section 24 deals with deductions.

Income from letting out vacant land is taxable under:
A) Income from salaries
B) Income from house property
C) Income from other sources
D) Income from business
Answer: C
Solution: Vacant land is not a building or house property, so rent from it falls under Income from Other Sources, not house property.

Standard deduction under Section 24(a) is:
A) 25% of net annual value
B) 30% of net annual value
C) 35% of net annual value
D) 40% of net annual value
Answer: B
Solution: Section 24(a) allows a flat 30% deduction on net annual value as standard deduction without requiring proof of actual expenditure.

Municipal taxes are deductible from:
A) Gross annual value
B) Net annual value
C) Fair rent
D) Standard rent
Answer: A
Solution: Municipal taxes paid by the owner are deducted from Gross Annual Value (GAV) to arrive at Net Annual Value (NAV). NAV = GAV - Municipal taxes.

The annual value of a self-occupied house is:
A) Market rent
B) Municipal value
C) Nil
D) Standard rent
Answer: C
Solution: For a self-occupied property used for own residence, the Annual Value is taken as Nil under Section 23(2). No income is deemed to arise from it.

Interest on borrowed capital for self-occupied property is deductible up to:
A) Rs. 1,00,000
B) Rs. 1,50,000
C) Rs. 2,00,000
D) Rs. 2,50,000
Answer: C
Solution: Under Section 24(b), interest on home loan for a self-occupied property is deductible up to Rs. 2,00,000 per year if construction is completed within 5 years.

Unrealized rent received later is taxable under:
A) Income from house property in the year received
B) Income from other sources
C) Capital gains
D) Business income
Answer: A
Solution: As per Section 25AA, unrealized rent subsequently received is taxable as Income from House Property in the year it is actually received, after 30% deduction.

Net annual value is calculated as:
A) Gross annual value + Municipal taxes
B) Gross annual value - Municipal taxes
C) Fair rent + Standard rent
D) Municipal value - Standard deduction
Answer: B
Solution: Net Annual Value (NAV) = Gross Annual Value (GAV) minus Municipal Taxes paid by the owner during the year.

Loss from house property can be set off against:
A) Only salary income
B) Any head of income up to Rs. 2,00,000
C) Only business income
D) Cannot be set off
Answer: B
Solution: Loss from house property can be set off against income from any other head, but only up to Rs. 2,00,000 in a year. Remaining loss is carried forward for 8 years.

Arrears of rent received are taxable after deduction of:
A) 10% of arrears
B) 25% of arrears
C) 30% of arrears
D) 40% of arrears
Answer: C
Solution: Under Section 25B, arrears of rent received are included in income after allowing a deduction of 30% of the arrears received. No other deductions are allowed.

Under Section 24(b) interest on loan for repair of house is deductible up to:
A) Rs. 30,000
B) Rs. 50,000
C) Rs. 1,50,000
D) Rs. 2,00,000
Answer: A
Solution: For repair/reconstruction of self-occupied house, interest deduction is limited to Rs. 30,000 only, not Rs. 2,00,000. The higher limit applies only to acquisition/construction.

The deemed owner concept applies when:
A) Property is transferred to spouse
B) Property is rented out
C) Property is self-occupied
D) Property is vacant
Answer: A
Solution: Under Section 27, a person who transfers a house to their spouse without adequate consideration is treated as the deemed owner and taxed on that property's income.

Co-owners of a property are assessed:
A) Jointly as a single unit
B) Individually based on their share
C) Only the first co-owner is assessed
D) Not assessed separately
Answer: B
Solution: Each co-owner is assessed individually on their proportionate share of the income from the property. They can each claim separate deductions under Section 24.

Fair rent means:
A) Rent fixed by municipality
B) Rent which a similar property would fetch
C) Actual rent received
D) Standard rent fixed by law
Answer: B
Solution: Fair rent is the rent which a similar property in the same locality would fetch in the open market. It is one of the factors used to determine Gross Annual Value.
